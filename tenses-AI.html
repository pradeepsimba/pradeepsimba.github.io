English Tenses Overview

Simple Present Tense

Sentence Type Formula Example
Affirmative I / you / we / they + main verb (base form)
He / she / it + main verb + s/es		 I play tennis.
She plays tennis.
Negative I / you / we / they + do not (don't) + main verb (base form)
He / she / it + does not (doesn't) + main verb (base form)		 I do not play tennis.
He doesn't play tennis.
Interrogative Do + I / you / we / they + main verb (base form)?
Does + he / she / it + main verb (base form)?		 Do you play tennis?
Does she play tennis?
Negative-Interrogative Do + I / you / we / they + not + main verb (base form)? (or Don't + ...)
Does + he / she / it + not + main verb (base form)? (or Doesn't + ...)		 Don't you play tennis?
Doesn't he play tennis?

Present Continuous Tense

Sentence Type Formula Example
Affirmative I + am + main verb + ing
He / She / It + is + main verb + ing
You / We / They + are + main verb + ing		 I am playing tennis.
She is playing tennis.
They are playing tennis.
Negative I + am not + main verb + ing
He / She / It + is not (isn't) + main verb + ing
You / We / They + are not (aren't) + main verb + ing		 I am not playing tennis.
He isn't playing tennis.
They aren't playing tennis.
Interrogative Am + I + main verb + ing?
Is + He / She / It + main verb + ing?
Are + You / We / They + main verb + ing?		 Am I playing tennis?
Is she playing tennis?
Are you playing tennis?
Negative-Interrogative Am + I + not + main verb + ing?
Is + He / She / It + not + main verb + ing? (or Isn't + ...)
Are + You / We / They + not + main verb + ing? (or Aren't + ...)		 Am I not playing tennis?
Isn't he playing tennis?
Aren't they playing tennis?

Has + he / she / it + past participle?		 Have you played tennis?

Negative-Interrogative Have + I / you / we / they + not + past participle? (or Haven't + ...)

Simple Past Tense

Sentence Type Formula Example
Affirmative Subject + Past Simple form (verb + ed *or* irregular form) I played tennis.
She went home.
Negative Subject + did not (didn't) + main verb (base form) I did not play tennis.
He didn't go home.
Interrogative Did + Subject + main verb (base form)? Did you play tennis?
Did she go home?
Negative-Interrogative Did + Subject + not + main verb (base form)? (or Didn't + ...) Didn't you play tennis?
Didn't he go home?
